  1. Definition

Bollinger bands trading is a mean-reversion method, which assumes that share price fluctuates around a specific level. We often use an SMA line to indicate the mean price. It attempts to buy shares when the price hits the lower band and sell them when it hits the upper band. Two examples of using 20-day Bollinger bands trading on Tesla and Toyota are shown in the figure below, where all the green (buy) signals lie on the bottom band, and all the red (sell) signals lie on the top band. 

Trading Tesla for two years (from 2019 to 2021) using this strategy would bring us $1.31, or a 1% profit. On the other hand, trading Toyota would give us $436, or a 4.3% profit.

  1. Pros and cons of Bollinger bands trading

    An advantage of Bollinger bands trading is that it is easy to understand and implement, as long as we understand the concept of standard deviation. Bollinger bands trading works well with stocks that are trading in a sideway trend, such as Toyota. 

    However, its greatest disadvantage lies in its assumption that share prices are always mean-reverting. Sometimes, a sudden negative price move means even traders who buy shares at the bottom Bollinger band might still not have an opportunity to close their position at a profit. Likewise, if the stock is ever-increasing (in Tesla’s case towards the end of 2020), the algorithm might not find an appropriate price point to open a new position, hence miss out on opportunities to make a profit.
