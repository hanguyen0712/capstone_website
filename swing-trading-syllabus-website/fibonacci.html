  1. Definition

Fibonacci retracement is a chart-based method for identifying possible support/resistance levels (Mitchell, 2020). It leverages the Fibonacci retracement levels (23.6%, 38.2%, 61.8%, 78.6%), which are derived from the famous Fibonacci sequence, to calculate key levels within a range of stock prices. Fibonacci retracement assumes that whether during an uptrend or a downtrend, share prices should move past each Fibonacci level and bounce back to it before continuing on with its current trend. 

  1. Use case

Fibonacci retracement is useful for placing entry orders, stop-loss orders, or setting price targets (Mitchell, 2020). Students unfamiliar with these terms can come back to the basic concept section to review.

  1. Limitations

Using Fibonacci retracement is an arbitrary process. It involves identifying two price levels on the stock chart as the 0% and 100% levels, and calculating three different percentages (the Fibonacci retracement levels) within that range. There is no clear guideline on how to identify these floor and ceiling levels, so it depends largely on investors’ intuition to determine which points are relevant. This method is thus not helpful to novice traders who might not have a good intuition of the stock market yet. 

On the other hand, this practice might be prone to confirmation bias as traders might be too confident that the price would move according to the Fibonacci levels. A failure case is demonstrated, also in Facebook price, in Q3 2018. Instead of decreasing gradually according to the Fibonacci levels, the share price plunged by 20% in one single day, meaning it crossed three consecutive Fibonacci levels. We could not have foreseen the significant dip following the Q2 earnings call before it occurred, and our prediction based on Fibonacci levels failed in this scenario. 

Conclusion: Fibonacci retracement is a popular swing trading method because it is easy to understand. However, it is also arbitrary and prone to confirmation bias. Thus, it cannot be used alone when formulating a swing trading strategy since the market is inherently unpredictable. We should, thus, use it only to inform our decision using other methods (presented below).
